December 2019 - Graduated from Iowa State University
I graduated from Iowa State University with a BS in Chemical Engineering. During my undergraduate studies I managed to maintain a 3.76 GPA.
I participated in various clubs and activities, such as the Society of Chemistry Undergraduate Majors (SCUM) and the Iowa State ChemE Car Team. I also completed several internships with Grain Processing Corporation in Muscatine, Iowa, gaining practical engineering experience in their Starch and Alcohol departments.
All these experiences, combined with my rigorous education, prepared me for my first industrial workplace.
March 2020 - Started working for Tate & Lyle
I began working for Tate & Lyle in the Pilot Plant in Decatur, IL as a Production Engineer in early March 2020. About 1 week later, the entire world shut down due to COVID-19.
While most of the seasoned engineers began working from home, I continued going into the plant to train and absorb whatever knowledge I could.
I'm now overseeing production, solving day-to-day problems within the facility, and specifying and installing new equipment as part of continuous improvement to our process. I've also been collaborating with Tate & Lyle's scientists to provide technical knowledge for new product development.
May 2022 - Began journey of self-taught programming
In May of 2022 I took an interest in computer science and coding, and decided to switch career paths. During the last 14 months I've been teaching myself how to write computer programs in my spare time after work for roughly 12 - 15 hours every week.
Most of my learning has been accomplished through books, online courses, and personal projects. I've completed a certificate for Scientific Computing with Python through freeCodeCamp.org, as well as Harvard's CS50x course.
Currently I'm working through The Odin Project, a community-sourced course aimed at teaching people how to become fullstack web developers. The tools and knowledge I've gained from that course is what I used to create this website for myself. I'm proud of how far I've come, and I'm excited to continue learning better ways to program!
